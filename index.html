Mission

For some people, education is not a choice. It is a chance at a better future. We hope to raise money for the Bernard and Elsie Weiss Dago Scholarship Fund, to sponser a child's highschool education, and significantly improve their quality of life.

For more info about Dago, visit www.hopefordago.org

Who are We?

Simply put, we are Xander Apponi, Chester Hall, and Sam Witz. With a passion to help and a little bit of crazy, we plan on biking across the country to raise money for students in Kenya. We believe that everyone should have the right to education, no matter what their circumstance may be.

The Plan

We depart at Yorktown, VA and begin heading west, then west, finally, west again. 3,700 miles later, we will end in San Francisco, then take a train back to Chicago.

How You Can Help

It costs about $3,000 for one student to receive a high school education. That includes room and board, tuition, books, uniforms, and fees. Help us bring HOPE to the children of Dago.

Dago, Kenya is a tiny village of 3,000 people in southwest Kenya where the average family income is less than $500 per year. The people live in mud huts and have no electricity or plumbing. Most children do not even make it to 8th grade, especially girls. Many families have no idea where their next meal is coming from, and AIDS has devastated this community.

Since the government of Kenya only pays through an eighth grade education, virtually the only students in Dago who get to go to high school are ones who are sponsored. For many years before our foundation, only a handful of Dago children ever went to high school.

This foundation is dramatically changing the lives of these students, giving them and their entire community of Dago real HOPE for a better future. These students will grow up, be successful and in turn help other Kenyan children the way they have been helped.
